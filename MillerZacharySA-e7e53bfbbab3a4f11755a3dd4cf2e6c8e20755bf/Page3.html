Thankfully, Jack’s fight-or-flight reflexes kicked in, saving him just in the nick of time as he stabbed his makeshift sword directly into the beast’s right eye, turning its face into a grotesque and bloody mess. It let out a louder scream that left Jack’s ears ringing, but before he could process what had just happened he knew he had to run. He ran and ran and ran until he could see a wall. The exit! The way out! He had beaten this terrible place once and for all. As he approached the wall, a feeling of dread began to set in, though. It was the feeling of realizing there was no door out of the bio-dome. As he was about to scream that they had tricked him, he felt a searing pain. The beast was back and had just ripped out his throat. As he lay there, lifeless, two scientists sat far above him in a room on top of the bio-dome.

“Test 217 complete,” one said to the other. “Prepare room 218 for the next subject.”

The End
