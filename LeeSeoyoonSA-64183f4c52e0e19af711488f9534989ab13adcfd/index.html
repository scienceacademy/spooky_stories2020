In the Night

“Reporting news, a serial killer is roaming at night picking on victims everyday. There would be a curfew from 9 pm to 6 am everyday, in order to protect our citizens. As you know, electricity is gone during the night, which will make the cities completely dark. Be careful and please follow the curfew."

“Hey, have you heard the news about the killer? I bet they’re lying to us to do something at night.”

“Yeah and I don’t think they would lie about that...a curfew isn’t just a joke, Mark.” I said.

“You’re too naive Mary. Do you honestly believe there’s a serial killer going around everyday to kill people? Do you realize how dumb that sounds?.” Mark scoffed.

“Mark! Are you serious? Who knows what will happen if you’re out there, in the dark, all alone, without any help? I won’t be part of this.” I relented.

“You’re such a baby..” Mark chuckled.

“Are you serious? You know that I can’t let you do that.” I sighed.

A day passed by quickly and I had no choice but to follow Mark. Although I was scared to go out, I followed Mark outside my door. The wind outside sounded more heavy today than the other days. It sounded like a ghost whispering in my ear. We had to use a thin match to light up our way. This match seemed like our only hope in the dark. Mark grinned as I walked towards him.

“Don’t worry, we’ll be fine. We’re going to head towards the capital and if we do get caught, just say that you got lost.” Mark reassured.

“Can’t we just go back? Please? I don’t have a good feeling about this.” I plead.

Mark ignored me and kept walking. As we kept walking, I saw a glimpse of a shadow at the corner of my eye. I heard a rustle behind me. My heart skipped a beat and I was shaking. When I looked ahead of me, Mark was gone. He was there a minute ago.

“Mark? Mark…” I whimpered, feeling my tears fill up my eyes. I was alone, in the dark, with a serial killer roaming around. I had no idea where I was..just trees, ground, and the streets. The burning match was my only hope in the dark. I couldn’t just stay at my spot so I decided to roam, to find Mark and go back home. I took a deep breath and then started walking back to where I thought was the direction to home. While I walked for about 5 minutes, I heard the same rustle, the same one I heard before.

I wanted to scream out loud like I did in the haunted house, that we went a few years ago. The horrible truth is that this isn’t fake and one scream can actually cause my death. I blew out my match so that the “shadow” wouldn’t see the light. I held my breath, trying to not make any noise. I felt a sneeze coming....at the worst timing possible! I tried to hold it but it came out anyway. Silence came after...only my thumping heart can be heard. I heard footsteps coming towards me. I froze like ice, my eyes wide open. The scent smelled familiar...blood! I remembered when I was almost killed when I was younger. A killer was in my house when I had no one. It eventually became a trauma to me which made me not feel safe being alone. My mind went blank.

Footsteps faded away and I felt myself coming back to my senses. I started to roam around, trying to look for hopeless help. I got used to the dark after blinking my eyes often. It was as if I had night vision but in a realistic way. After walking a certain distance, I saw..a body. It had blood everywhere...I ran toward the body.

“Hello? Are you okay?” I whispered.

I turned the body over and I saw a familiar face. No way...It’s Mark! Oh no...I started sobbing, not knowing what to do anymore. I regretted coming out with Mark. I just wish everything that’s happening is just me dreaming…There’s no way this can be true…

I felt a poke on my back, like a real sharp one. Is it a knife? I couldn’t breathe and the only thing I heard was a chuckle behind me. Honestly, the chuckle was scarier than the actual act happening. I felt my eyes close…

The next thing I saw happening was a white ceiling...is this a hospital room? I’m..alive, but where’s Mark? I wanted to shout out loud and look for Mark everywhere.

“Where’s Mark?” I asked, desperately.

The nurse was surprised to see me wake up. She called the doctors and shouted for help.

“She finally woke up after a year! This is a true miracle. How do you feel, Mary?” a doctor asked. “Please...tell me where Mark is.” I repeated.

“Um..who’s Mark? There’s no one in your guardian list called that. Maybe you were dreaming.”

I was..dreaming? About Mark and everything...no way. I refused to believe it. I ran out the hospital room, ignoring everyone’s calls and the outside. Electricity was back.
